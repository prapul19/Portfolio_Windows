Project
ZigMa

ZigMa

Designing a Career Progress Ecosystem for Job Seekers and Recruiters

An integrated mobile platform enabling users to learn skills, build professional identity, discover relevant opportunities, and connect with recruiters — all within a unified experience.

Open Prototype in Figma

Quick snapshot

Platform: Mobile

Role: Product / UX Designer

Scope: End-to-end experience design

Focus: Career discovery · Job application · Networking · Profile systems

Context

Modern job seekers navigate multiple disconnected platforms to build their careers — learning skills on one platform, networking on another, and applying to opportunities elsewhere. This fragmented journey creates friction, reduces confidence, and delays career progress. ZigMa explores how a unified mobile ecosystem can support users throughout their professional lifecycle by bringing learning, opportunity discovery, and recruiter interaction into a single structured experience.

Problem statement

Users struggle with:

  • Lack of clear transition from learning skills to applying for jobs
  • Difficulty maintaining structured professional identity across platforms
  • Limited contextual networking opportunities
  • Inefficient job filtering and application tracking
  • Recruiters face challenges identifying candidate readiness beyond static resume data

Design objective

The goal was to design a progress-driven career platform that: encourages continuous professional development, simplifies opportunity discovery through intelligent filtering, enables meaningful candidate–recruiter interactions, and provides visibility into user growth and readiness.

Signature product insight

Rather than designing ZigMa as a traditional job listing application, the experience was shaped around the idea that career growth is a continuous journey rather than a single hiring event. This led to designing interconnected flows where users can move seamlessly from learning → profile building → networking → opportunity discovery → application. This progression-based model aims to increase motivation and reduce decision uncertainty.

Screen captions

  • Onboarding & Authentication — Simple entry flow designed to reduce initial friction and encourage quick platform exploration.
  • Home Dashboard — A personalised overview combining learning cues, recommended jobs, and recent activity signals to support career momentum.
  • Job Discovery & Filtering — Structured filtering system enabling users to explore opportunities based on skills, specialisation, and career preferences.
  • Job Detail Evaluation — Information hierarchy designed to support confident decision-making through clear role expectations, company insights, and application actions.
  • Profile Builder — Modular profile architecture allowing users to progressively add education, skills, experience, and achievements — reinforcing identity clarity.
  • Social Feed & Networking — Professional interaction layer encouraging peer learning, alumni connection, and visibility within the ecosystem.
  • Recruiter Job Posting Flow — Simplified workflow enabling organisations to publish opportunities efficiently while maintaining structured job metadata.
  • Messaging & Communication — Direct interaction channel supporting faster decision cycles between candidates and recruiters.
  • Application & Resume Upload — Guided submission flow designed to reduce abandonment and improve task completion clarity.

Outcome

ZigMa demonstrates how integrating fragmented career touchpoints into a single mobile ecosystem can enhance perceived user progress, improve engagement, and support more confident job-seeking behaviour. The project highlights the importance of designing not only for task completion but for long-term professional motivation.

Key learnings

  • Designing complex ecosystems requires clear hierarchy between primary and supporting user goals.
  • Career platforms benefit from progress visibility rather than static listing experiences.
  • Networking features significantly influence platform stickiness.
  • System-level thinking is essential when multiple user roles coexist.
